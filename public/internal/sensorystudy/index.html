Camera v0.3
The thesis shift

Delivering on eyes in the world required moving from a 50mm baseline to 30mm / 75° FOV — capturing peripheral presence rather than directed attention. The device lives in the hand, not at the eye.

Orientationless capture means the output must be 1:1 square, cropped from the sensor's image circle regardless of how the device is held. These two constraints together — wide FOV + square crop — eliminate the IMX585 as the primary sensor and point toward a larger format.

01 — The spec chain
How one decision drives every other
Every number downstream of "what does the image need to feel like" is deterministic. This is the chain.
Target quality bar ARRI / Mamiya RB67 Optical character, tonal transitions, dynamic range without flash
Capture geometry 1:1 square, orientationless Sensor must yield equal H and V resolution in any rotation
Field of view ~75° diagonal 30mm full-frame equivalent — peripheral, not directed
Min square crop MP ≥ 8 MP Below 6MP feels like a phone. 8MP+ is undeniable at output size.
Required sensor short axis ≥ 9mm At 2.0µm pitch: 9mm ÷ 0.002mm = 4,500px → 20MP square
Required image circle ≥ 18mm √2 × 9mm × 1.15 margin = 14.6mm min, 18mm for quality corners
Actual focal length ~20–22mm 30mm equiv on a ~1" sensor (crop factor ~1.4×)
Optical column diameter ~26–30mm Image circle + housing. Must fit within 55mm hourglass body.

05 — Forward paths
Three options for Monday
01
Ship IMX585, accept the crop
4.75MP square crop. Bet on Golgi pipeline and computational photography to close the quality gap. Fastest to hardware, lowest engineering risk. The body and optics you've already scoped work. Honest downside: at output size, it will not feel like the best capture device ever made. That matters for Camera.
Fastest · Quality compromise
02
IMX989, solve the thermal + body
20MP square crop at 3.2µm effective pixels. The best handheld square capture that has ever existed. Engineering challenges are real: thermal management, power, body geometry, driver work. But they are finite problems. This is the product Camera needs to be to be undeniable.
Hardest · Strongest product
03
Custom sensor target for Series A
Define the ideal: 1/1.1" format, 2.0µm native, tall aspect ratio for square crop efficiency. Approach Sony Semiconductor semi-custom program. Not realistic at Seed — but all decisions made now should preserve this path. Ship IMX989 at v1, custom sensor at v2.
Long horizon · Right destination
The question for Monday

Is thermal + power a product engineering problem we commit to solving — or a reason to ship a camera that doesn't meet the quality bar we've described to ourselves?

Path 02 is harder. It's also the only one that delivers on the thesis.

The procurement reality for Camera at Seed

The IMX585 and IMX678 can be ordered today, one unit, no relationship required. The IMX989 cannot. It lives in a mobile OEM supply chain built for companies ordering millions of units annually. Getting it as a Seed-stage hardware startup means either going through Chinese module suppliers with no Sony support (supply chain risk, no driver stack, warranty exposure) or establishing a direct Sony SSC relationship — which requires demonstrating volume commitments that don't exist yet.

This is the actual blocker for Path 02, not the thermal engineering. The question for Monday is whether Kinship can establish a Sony SSC relationship now — through investor intros, through the Sigma/Håkan channel, or through a strategic partnership — that gets IMX989 access ahead of production volumes.
